Curriculum, Academic, and Learning Analytics ‚Äì What is the Difference?

Curriculum analytics involves evaluating and enhancing an educational institution's curriculum. Academic analytics encompasses a broader range of data analysis related to institutional effectiveness, while learning analytics focuses on individual student learning experiences.

All fields use data to improve education but differ in scope, target, and data sources. Curriculum analytics optimises curriculum design, academic analytics covers broader institutional aspects, and learning analytics concentrates on personalised student learning.
